Key Quranic Dialogues Between Allah and Iblis
Refusal to Bow & Arrogance (Surah Al-A'raf 7:12-13):
Allah: "What prevented you from bowing down when I commanded you?"
Iblis: "I am better than him. You created me from fire and him from clay" (7:12).
Allah: "Descend from this place! It is not for you to be arrogant here. Get out! You are humiliated" (7:13).
The Request for Respite (Surah Al-Hijr 15:36-43):
Iblis: "My Lord, then respite me until the Day they are resurrected" (15:36).
Allah: "You are indeed of those allowed respite" (15:37).
Iblis: "Because You have put me in error, I will surely sit for them on Your straight path. Then I will come to them from before them and from behind them, from their right and from their left, and You will not find most of them grateful" (15:16-17).
The Threat of Hell (Surah Al-A'raf 7:18):
Allah: "Get out of here, disgraced and rejected! Whoever follows you, I will fill Hell with all of you!"
Satan's Limited Power (Surah Al-Hijr 15:42):
Allah: "You will have no authority over My servants, except for those who follow you from among the deviators." 
Key Quranic Ayat Describing the Conflict
Surah Al-Isra 17:61-65: Mentions Iblis saying he will dominate Adam's descendants, but Allah informs him he has no authority over sincere servants.
Surah Sad 38:71-85: Details the arrogance of Iblis and his vow to mislead humans, ending with Allah's promise to fill Hell with Iblis and his followers.
Surah Ibrahim 14:22: Reveals that on the Day of Judgment, Satan will admit: "Allah made you a true promise... I had no authority over you except that I called you, and you obeyed me. So do not blame me; blame yourselves." 
Key Themes in the Conversation
Arrogance (Kibr): The root cause of Iblis's downfall (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:34).
Jealousy: Iblis's envy of Adam's status.
Divine Decree: Allah knew what Iblis would do, using it to test humanity.
Free Will: Humans have the choice to follow the straight path or to follow Satan's temptations.
